Engineering Technology is focused more on the implementation of solutions relevant to engineering, rather than developing new methods and solutions. These careers tend to be more 'hands-on', rather than research, development, or design. There are many subfields of Engineering Technology, but two prominent subfields are listed below:

Computer Engineering Technology

Computer Engineering Technologists are employed as embedded software technologists, computer support specialists, networking support specialists, automation engineers, applications engineers, telecommunications engineers, network support technical engineers, and network administrators. Computer Engineering Technologists are employed by industry, academia and government labs for manufacturing and analysis.

Career Outlook

Median salary: $81,785 (Burning Glass)
Projected growth: 10% from 2020 to 2030 (NACE)

Lean Systems Engineering Technology

Lean Systems Engineering Technologists are employed as Lean Continuous Improvement Engineers, Lean Engineers,  Manufacturing Engineers, Lean Process Improvement Engineers, Supplier Quality Engineers, Lean Manufacturing Specialists and Quality Engineers. They are employed by the manufacturing and non-manufacturing industries, nongovernmental organizations, and government labs for manufacturing and analysis.

Career Outlook

>

Median salary: $79,744 (Burning Glass)
Projected growth: 10% from 2020 to 2030 (NACE)

  • Consulting firms
  • Governmental agencies
  • Energy and environmental solutions
