Aerospace Engineering is the filed that is concerned with the development of aircrafts and spacecrafts. There are two branches, aeronautical and astronautical engineering. Aeronautical focuses on designing aircrafts like planes, jets, helicopters, etc. Astronautical focuses on developing and desgining spacecrafts. Aerospace engineering focuses on designing large machinery such as aircrafts, spacescrafts, satellites, and missles. They also test the protypes of this large machinery. Aerospace Engineers also perfrom destructive and non-destructive testing for strength, functionality, reliability, and long-term durabiltiy of the aircrafts and parts they design. The main areas of focus for Aerospace engineers include aerodynamics and knowledge of aircraft power plants. Additionlly, Astronautical engineers have to understand spacecraft propulsion, including thermodynamics. Aerospace engineering requires many skills overlapped by many other disciplines. These skills include physics, mathematics, aerodynamics, materials science, computer-aided design (CAD), and computer simulations.
